About Plans Zones Team

Talk with Focus and Privacy

In the Harvard Law School Library, we regularly observe students and staff migrate to the stairwells and other border zones to make phone and video calls. These locations are not ideal. They compromise focus and privacy, and can be distracting to others. This project, Private Talking Spaces, is an effort to create ligthweight, positive environments for phone conversations in libraries, musems, and open offices.

Private Talking Spaces are furniture objects that are assembled in your existing space. They’re freestanding, secure, soundproof, and well engineered. The Harvard Law School Library is installing two spaces in August 2017 and we’re looking for early partners.

Mixed: mobile and desktop

The Bootstrap v4 grid system has five tiers of classes: xs (extra small), sm (small), md (medium), lg (large), and xl (extra large). You can use nearly any combination of these classes to create more dynamic and flexible layouts.

Each tier of classes scales up, meaning if you plan on setting the same widths for xs and sm, you only need to specify xs.
