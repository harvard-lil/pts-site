Private Talking Spaces

enable people to conduct audio and video calls in public spaces

This is an effort to create occupiable furniture that provide aural privacy to people in libraries, museums, and open offices.

Talk with Focus and Privacy

In the Harvard Law School Library, we regularly observe students and staff migrate to the stairwells and other border areas to make phone and video calls.

These locations are not ideal. They compromise focus and privacy, and can be distracting to others.

This project, Private Talking Spaces, is an effort to create ligthweight, positive environments for phone conversations in libraries, musems, and open offices.

Private Talking Spaces are ocupiable furniture that are assembled in your existing space.

They’re freestanding, secure, soundproof, and well engineered.

The Harvard Law School Library is installing two spaces in August 2017 and we’re looking for early partners.

Plans and Technical Details

Private Talking Spaces are

  • assembled in your space and are relocatable with a few hours of labor
  • freestanding with internal lighting powered by a common wall outlet
  • secure, thanks to the large windows and high quality wood/acrylic constuction

Two or More Creates a Zone

Private Talking Spaces are designed to sit next to each other. Positioning two or more together forms a talking zone.

Talking zones have tremendous gravity and are a clear draw when folks are moving quickly to find a space to make a call.

Designers   Librarians   Engineers

In April 2016, Private Talking Spaces came to life in two birthing rooms, the Harvard Library Innovation Lab and the Work-Shop Design Studio.
