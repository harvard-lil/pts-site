Focus and Privacy

Private Talking Spaces are freestanding, soundproof environments that provide aural privacy to people in libraries, museums, and open offices.

The spaces are designed to sit next to each other. Positioning two or more together creates a gravity that draws folks in.

Endless Benefits

Open working environments overexpose us. Too many sounds. Too many distractions. They force us to multi-task and keep us from being our best selves.

When we increase our privacy, we perform at significanly higher levels. We become more creative, our cognitive abilities improve, and our stress levels decrease. Our sick days drop and we add 15% to the bottom line.

The benefits of working in a focused space are clear and long established, but securing the funding and slicing through the red tape of altering physical infrastructure can be an organizational challenging.

Private Talking Spaces are a lightweight solution and are installed without alterations to your building's infrastructure. Pop them up in your space and instantly add an environment that encourages focus, happiness, and productivity.

Occupiable Furniture

Internal lighting, power outlets, a bench, and a desk provide comfort and promote mental focus.

The spaces can be relocated with about four hours of labor and require nothing more than a standard wall outlet for power.

Smart Spaces

Sensors inside the spaces help you understand community engagement.

Historic occupancy, temperature, and humidity readings are always available to you and your web browser.

We're with You

Private Talking Spaces started in the Harvard Law School Library. We regularly observed students and staff migrating to the stairwells and other border areas to make phone and video calls.

Partnering with Work-Shop, the Law School Library, created Private Talking Spaces and shared their open documentation enabling you make your own. But, if you don't have access to a fab lab, let us professionally build one for you.
