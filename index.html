Focus and Privacy

Private Talking Spaces are freestanding, soundproof environments that provide aural privacy to people in libraries, museums, and open offices.

They're a lightweight solution and are installed without alterations to your building's infrastructure.

Private Talking Spaces started in the Harvard Library Innovation Lab. Leverage the Lab's open documentation to make your own, or let us build one for you.

Occupiable Furniture

Internal lighting, power outlets, a bench, and a desk provide comfort and promote mental focus.

Smart Spaces

Sensors inside the space continually monitor movement and enviornemntal states to help you understand engagement.

The spaces can be relocated with about four hours of labor and require nothing more than a standard wall outlet for power.

Historic occupancy and environmental readings are always available to you and your web browser.
