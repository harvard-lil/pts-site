Focus and Privacy

Private Talking Spaces are occupiable furniture. They're assembled in your existing space and require no alterations to your building's infrastructure.

They're freestanding, soundproof, and provide a place for mental focus and aural privacy to people in libraries, museums, and open offices.

Private Talking Spaces were created in the Harvard Library Innovation Lab to provide places for students and staff to conduct interviews and make sensitive calls.

If you have access to a fab lab, you can build your own spaces using the open construction docs - let us know how to help.

Smart Spaces

Private Talking Spaces are equipped with sensors to measure when a person is sitting inside.

Historic sensor readings are available in your web browser to help you understand how your community are engaging with the spaces.
