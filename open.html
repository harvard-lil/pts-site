Private Talking Spaces

enable people to conduct audio and video calls in open environments

In the Harvard Law School Library, we regularly observe students and staff migrate to the stairwells and other border areas to make phone and video calls.

These locations are not ideal. They compromise focus and privacy, and can be distracting to others.

This project, Private Talking Spaces, is an effort to create ligthweight, positive environments for phone conversations in libraries, musems, and open offices.
